Mission

To save lives one wing at a time
Dedicated to search and rescue recovery, marketed for government agencies and disaster relief organizations.

Why

Civilian drones have been used to save at least 59 people in 18 different incidents around the world since 2013.
In the days after Hurricane Harvey, the Federal Aviation Administration issued 137 airspace authorizations for drone-related recover efforts in the Houston area. After Hurricane Irma, the FAA issued 132 airspace authorizations in the Florida area.
Artificial intelligence is transforming humankind.
The Eagle soars over devastated areas, detecting victims in the aftermath of a calamity when rescue teams are futile in providing aid.

How

Technology such as ours has already been tested in Cape May, New Jersey where emergency supplies were delivered to a boat off the Jersey shore. In fact the United Nations supports the use of drones in disaster relief "[Drone delivery] allows for instant humanitarian relief within minutes and without having to worry about logistics". We believe that we can expand the use of this technology to empower communities with the peace of mind that help is indeed there in the event of a natural disaster.

When

The FAA opened up skies for drone operators in 2017 when it legalized commercial drone use through its Part 107 rules.
Our technology will soon be accessible to communities across the world.
